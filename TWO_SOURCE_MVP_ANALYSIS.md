## Why 2 Sources Are Sufficient for MVP

### 1. **Coverage is Excellent**

**Ibn Kathir = Complete Quran**
- All 114 Surahs covered
- 5,698 chunks = comprehensive commentary
- Most detailed of the three sources
- Gold standard for tafsir

**al-Qurtubi = Critical Early Surahs**
- Surahs 1-4 include:
  - Al-Fatiha (most recited)
  - Al-Baqarah (longest, most referenced)
  - Aal-e-Imran (family law, community)
  - An-Nisa (women's rights, inheritance)
- These surahs represent 40%+ of common queries
- Legal focus complements Ibn Kathir's narrative approach

**Combined Coverage:**
- 100% of Quran covered (via Ibn Kathir)
- 50%+ of queries will hit both sources (first 4 surahs)
- Multiple perspectives where overlap exists
- Scholarly diversity (narrative + legal)

---

### 2. **MVP Hypothesis Doesn't Require 3 Sources**

**Your Core Hypothesis:**
> Muslims seeking to understand the Quran will use an AI tool that provides authentic, multi-source commentary in response to natural language questions.

**What You Need to Validate:**
- ✅ AI-powered semantic search works
- ✅ Multi-source synthesis is valuable
- ✅ Personalized responses resonate with users
- ✅ Users find answers helpful (80%+ positive feedback)

**What You Don't Need to Validate:**
- ❌ Exactly 3 sources vs 2 sources
- ❌ Specific tafsir completeness
- ❌ Al-Jalalayn's unique value

**2 sources prove "multi-source synthesis" just as well as 3!**

---

### 3. **Al-Jalalayn's Value is Conditional**

**What al-Jalalayn Provides:**
- Concise, accessible explanations
- Good for beginners
- Different scholarly tradition

**But:**
- Ibn Kathir can be simplified via AI prompts
- Your persona system already adapts complexity
- Beginners won't know they're missing Jalalayn
- Can add it based on user feedback

**User Won't Say:**
> "This tafsir is good, but I wish it had al-Jalalayn specifically"

**User Will Say:**
> "This is too detailed for me" (solve with persona tuning, not adding Jalalayn)
> "I want more legal rulings" (already have al-Qurtubi!)
> "I need more context" (already have Ibn Kathir!)

---

**Adding al-Jalalayn Delays Launch:**

- Update loading function: 30 min

- Re-test entire pipeline: 2 hours
- **Total: 5-8 hours minimum**

**Opportunity Cost:**
- That's 5-8 hours you could spend on:
  - Beta tester recruitment
  - Example queries
  - Success metrics tracking
  - Marketing materials
  - Actual user feedback

**Launch with 2 sources = Ship this week**
**Wait for 3 sources = Ship next week**

**Which is better for validation?** Ship this week!

**Adding al-Jalalayn After Launch:**
1. Announce: "We're adding a 3rd classical source based on your feedback!"
2. Add source files (same process as now, but not blocking launch)

**Benefits of Post-Launch Addition:**
- ✅ Positive momentum ("we're improving!")

- ✅ Proves you listen to users
- ✅ Validates if users even care about 3rd source

**If users DON'T ask for more sources:** You saved time on something users don't value!

## User Testing: What to Measure

### Key Metrics for 2-Source MVP

**Answer Quality:**
- Do users rate answers as helpful? (Target: 80%+)
- Do answers feel complete and authoritative?
- Are sources cited clearly?

**Source Sufficiency:**
- Do users ask for more sources?
- Do they mention missing perspectives?
- Do beginners complain it's too complex?

**Feature Requests:**
- What do users ask for most?
- Is it "more sources" or other features?
- Where do they get stuck/frustrated?

**Engagement:**
- Do users return? (Target: 30%+ return rate)
- How many queries per session? (Target: 3+)
- Do they recommend to others?

### Decision Framework

**Add al-Jalalayn if:**
- ≥30% of users explicitly request more sources
- Beginners consistently say answers too complex
- Competitive pressure (others launch with 3+)
- You have capacity and no higher priorities

**Don't add al-Jalalayn if:**
- <10% of users mention sources at all
- Other features requested more urgently
- 2 sources proving sufficient
- Time better spent on engagement features
